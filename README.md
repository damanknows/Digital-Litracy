## 🧠 Section A: Establishing Digital Context
In the modern academic landscape, it is critical to distinguish between "tool familiarity" and true "digital understanding."

* **The Illusion of Competence:** Comfort with platforms like Canva or AI-generated suggestions can often mask a lack of technical critical thinking.
* **The Definition of Literacy:** Digital literacy is not just operating a tool; it is understanding the consequences of our digital actions, such as data privacy, copyright issues, and originality.
* **The Convenience Trap:** Students must move beyond being "consumers of digital convenience" and instead become critical thinkers who question the content they create and share.

## 🛡️ Section C: Cyber Safety & Responsibility

### The Psychological Gap
Academic awareness does not always translate into real-world caution. Cybercriminals succeed by exploiting human emotions like urgency (Temporal Pressure) and aspiration (Confirmation Bias).

### 8 Actionable Safety Tips for Students
1. **Never Share UPI PIN for Receiving Money:** A PIN is only required to *send* money or check your balance.
2. **Verify "Collect Requests":** Double-check the requester’s name on apps like GPay or PhonePe before approving any payment.
3. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on all academic, professional, and social accounts.
4. **Use a Password Manager:** Avoid using the same password for VITyarthi and your personal accounts; use unique, complex passwords.
5. **Audit App Permissions:** Regularly revoke unnecessary camera, microphone, or contact access for simple applications.
6. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Always use mobile data for financial transactions to prevent data interception.
7. **Mask Your Aadhaar:** Use the "Masked Aadhaar" option from the UIDAI website when providing identity verification.
8. **Think Before You Click:** If you receive an "urgent" SMS about a blocked SIM or KYC update, verify it through an official app instead of clicking the link.
